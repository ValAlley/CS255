Project Summary
The DriverPass project involved designing a comprehensive online management system for a driving school client. The primary goal was to transition their manual, paper-based processes into a centralized digital platform. The system was designed to handle student registrations, instructor scheduling, lesson tracking, and payment processing, providing a seamless interface for both the staff and the students.

Reflection on Performance
I believe I excelled in the architectural mapping and UML modeling phase. By breaking down the system into clear Use Case and Class diagrams, I was able to visualize the relationships between different entities—such as students, instructors, and administrative staff—ensuring that the logic was sound before any technical implementation began. This structural clarity helped identify potential bottlenecks in the scheduling flow early in the design process.

Areas for Revision
If I were to revise one specific part of the documentation, I would focus on the Gantt chart and project timeline. While the initial version provided a solid roadmap, I would improve it by incorporating more granular "buffer zones" for the testing and QA phases. In complex system designs, unexpected integration issues often arise, and a more conservative timeline would better reflect the iterative nature of software development.

Interpreting User Needs
To interpret the user’s needs, I focused on identifying the pain points of the current manual system, such as double-booked lessons and lost paperwork. I translated these into functional requirements, like automated conflict checking and secure cloud storage.
Prioritizing the user's needs is vital because a system's success is measured by its usability and adoption. If the design does not align with the actual workflow of the instructors or the ease of use expected by students, the system will fail to provide value, regardless of how technically advanced the backend might be.

pproach to Software Design
My approach to software design is rooted in iterative refinement. I prefer starting with high-level requirements and progressively drilling down into specific technical constraints. In the future, I plan to utilize Agile Methodologies, Prototyping, and Modular Design.
